FAQ: A Check of Benford's Law

By Chris Caldwell

As discussed on another page, Benford's lawstates that for many data distributions that the string of leading digits d occur with relative frequency

log10(1 + 1/d).
So the entries would begin with the leading digits 43 with frequency log10(1 + 1/43), or about 1.00% percent of the time.

To test this with a sample, on 21 December 2001, Andy Steward mailed me the results from his collection of factors of generalized repunitnumbers.  These include results from the Cunningham projectas well as Richard Brent's (and others) factor tables.  His list of 926,952 prime factors of generalized repunits with at least 5 digits yielded the following excellent fit.

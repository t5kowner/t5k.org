A 36,007 bit "nearly random" prime

This is a prime that had historic value.  Creating primes like this is easier now.

This 10,839 digit prime does not have a nice short description!  Preda Mihailescu formed it by computing a sequence of primes pi where prime is a "random" number times twice the proceeding prime plus one (see [Mihailescu94] and our pages on proving primality).  Below you will find one of his notes announcing this prime and the complete expansion in hexadecimal.

From: Preda Mihailescu
Date: Mon, 29 Dec 1997 21:10:12 +0100 (MET)
To: caldwell@utm.EdU
Subject: Re: Mersenne cofactors, personal note, etc.

While new records with cyclotomy are being boiled, I have another kind of large prime for you: a 36007 bits almost random, proved prime. Obviously, it does not look nice at all and you cannot describe by any other short cut than its value itself: I built it for this very purpose, to get away from the very specially shaped primes in the larger size domain. The prime was built and proved using AP, my algorithm for building cryptographic provable primes (CRYPTO94). I attach the file.
